# CS-230 Portfolio Reflection – The Gaming Room

## Briefly summarize The Gaming Room client and their software requirements.
The Gaming Room was a client that wanted to expand their game, Draw It or Lose It, from Android to a web-based application that could support multiple platforms. The software needed to allow multiple teams and players while making sure each game had unique names and could support growth across different systems.

## What did you do particularly well in developing this documentation?
I believe I did well explaining the platform recommendations and comparing different operating systems. I also explained the importance of security, scalability, and memory management when designing the application.

## What about the process of working through a design document did you find helpful when developing the code?
The design document helped me organize my ideas before coding. It helped me understand the project requirements and gave me a plan to follow during development.

## If you could revise one part of your work, what would you improve?
If I could revise one part, I would improve my operating platform evaluation by adding more details about cloud computing and modern deployment options.

## How did you interpret the user’s needs and implement them into your software design?
I focused on creating a design that supported multiple users, strong performance, and security. It was important to understand the client’s needs so the software would solve their problem effectively.

## How did you approach designing software?
I approached software design by reviewing requirements first and breaking the project into smaller parts. In the future, I will continue using planning documents, UML diagrams, and requirement analysis to improve my design process.
